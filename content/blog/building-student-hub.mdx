When I started my engineering degree, I quickly realized a massive gap: opportunity inequality. Students at top-tier universities had access to incredible hackathons, mentorship, and resources, while the rest of us were left to figure it out alone.

So, I created a WhatsApp group. I called it **Student Hub**.

## The Early Days

The first 100 members were just my classmates and friends from other colleges. I spent hours manually scraping hackathon platforms, LinkedIn, and university boards to post 2-3 high-quality opportunities every single day. 

> "Consistency builds trust. When people know they can rely on you for value, they stay."

## The Tipping Point

The community exploded when I built the **Student Hub Web App**. It transitioned us from a noisy chat group to a structured platform. 

We added features like:
- **Opportunity Boards:** Categorized by Internships, Hackathons, and Open Source.
- **Resource Libraries:** Curated roadmaps for AI/ML, Full-Stack, and Mobile development.
- **Success Stories:** Showcasing members who won hackathons or landed internships to inspire others.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Value First, Always:** Don't ask for engagement; provide value first. Engagement follows naturally.
2. **Empower Leaders:** I couldn't run a 5,000-member community alone. I identified active members and made them moderators and domain leads.
3. **Celebrate Small Wins:** When a member submitted their first pull request or won a local college event, we celebrated it like a major victory.

## The Future of Student Hub

Today, we have members from over 200 colleges across India. We've seen members win the Smart India Hackathon, get selected for GSoC, and land roles at FAANG companies. But the mission remains the same: democratize access to tech opportunities.
